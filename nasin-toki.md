### Simple Nouns
*(nimi suli lawa pona)*

| [‚Üë](#nouns) |
|:-|
| [Pronouns](#pronouns) |

**Simple nouns** don't have an additional meaning as **modifiers** or **verbs**.

| ijo | jan |
|:-|:-|
| *something, a thing* | *somebody, a person* |

#### Pronouns
*(nimi suli lawa esun)*

| [‚Üë](#simple-nouns) |
|:-|

**Pronouns** are **simple nouns** that refer to the speaker, the listener or a third person/object.

| mi | sina | ona |
|:-|:-|:-|
| *I, we* | *you* | *she, he, it, they* |

### Thing Nouns
*(nimi suli lawa ijo)*

| [‚Üë](#nouns) |
|:-|
| [Correlatives](#correlatives) |
| [Spatial Nouns](#spatial-nouns) |
| [Life-Form Nouns](#life-form-nouns) |

**Thing nouns** can imply the word "ijo".

| sike |
|:-|
| *round* ‚Üí *something round, circle, globe* |

**Nouns** that describe food seem to imply "ijo".

| moku | pan | soweli |
|:-|:-|:-|
| *mouthful* ‚Üí *food* | *seed* ‚Üí *bread* | *mammal* ‚Üí *meat* |

üëé The [modifiers](#modifiers) (or [prepositions](#prepositions)) "ante" and "sama" can become **thing nouns**.

| ijo ante ‚Üí ante | ijo sama ‚Üí sama |
|:-|:-|
| *something different* | *something similar* |

#### Correlatives
*(nimi suli lawa palisa)*

| [‚Üë](#thing-nouns) |
|:-|

**Correlatives** are **thing nouns**, i.e. they can imply the word "ijo".

| ale |
|:-|
| *every* ‚Üí *everything* |

In a genitive clause (i.e. the words behind the **particle** "[pi](#genitive)"), "ijo" before a **correlative** reappears.

| mama pi ijo ale | mama ale |
|:-|:-|
| *parent of everything* | *every parent* |

üëç A **subordinate clause** can be thought of as an extension of the **correlative** "ni".

| suno pi mute ni: mi ken ala lape |
|:-|
| *so much light, that I can't sleep* |

üëç In a **relative clause** (i.e. a **subordinate clause** with "ona ni"), "ona ni li" and "e ona ni" can disappear.

| mi lukin e nena ni: (ona ni li) suli mute. | nena ni: mi lukin (e ona ni), li suli. |
|:-|:-|
| *I'm seeing the mountain, that is big.* | *The mountain, that I'm seeing, is big.* |

üëç Questions with the **correlative** "seme" can be thought of **subordinate clauses** with an omitted **main clause** "o toki e ni".

| o toki e ni: mani li lon seme. ‚Üí mani li lon seme? |
|:-|
| *Tell me where the money is.* ‚Üí *Where is the money?* |

üëé The beginning of a **subordinate clause** can disappear. Therefore, [pre-verbs](#pre-verbs) and [prepositions](#prepositions) can become [nouns](#nouns).

| jan pi ona li tawa tomo | jan pi li tawa tomo | jan pi tawa tomo |
|:-|:-|:-|
| *somebody, who is going home* | *home-coming person* | *home-comer* |

ü§î According to **Sonja**, "seme" is a [particle](#particles), even though it behaves just like the other **correlatives**.

#### Spatial Nouns
*(nimi suli lawa nasin)*

| [‚Üë](#thing-nouns) |
|:-|

**Spatial nouns** are **thing nouns**, with an implied "ijo" meaning "location", "place".

| insa |
|:-|
| *inner* ‚Üí *inner area, center* |

üëç When a [verb](#verbs) becomes a **noun**, it can mean the place of its accomplishment.

| mi lon lape. | sina tan lape. | ona li tawa lape. |
|:-|:-|:-|
| *I am resting.* | *You have been resting.* | *She is going to rest.* |

üëç The [pre-verbs](#pre-verbs) "awen", "kama", "pini" can become **spatial nouns**.

| lon awen | lon kama | lon pini |
|:-|:-|:-|
| *during* | *before* | *after* |

ü§î ‚Ä¶

| lawa ‚Üí sewi |
|:-|
| *upper* ‚Üí *upper area, top* |

ü§î "lon awen pi", "lon kama pi", "lon pini pi" can become "lon".

| lon awen pi tenpo lili ‚Üí lon tenpo lili | lon kama pi tenpo lili ‚Üí lon tenpo lili | lon pini pi tenpo lili ‚Üí lon tenpo lili |
|:-|:-|:-|
| *shortly/quickly* | *provisionally* | *soon* |

#### Life-Form Nouns
*(nimi suli lawa sijelo)*

| [‚Üë](#thing-nouns) |
|:-|

**Life-form nouns** are **thing nouns**, with an implied "ijo" meaning "life-form".

üëç **Life-form nouns** imply the word "sijelo", meaning "life-form".

| akesi |
|:-|
| *cold-blooded* ‚Üí *reptile*, *amphibian* |

### Person Nouns
*(nimi suli lawa jan)*

| [‚Üë](#nouns) |
|:-|

**Person nouns** imply "jan".

| meli |
|:-|
| *female* ‚Üí *somebody female*, *woman* |

### Tool Nouns
*(nimi suli lawa ilo)*

| [‚Üë](#tool-nouns) |
|:-|

**Tool nouns** imply "ilo".

| lukin |
|:-|
| *to see* ‚Üí *eye* |

"pona (tawa)" + **tool noun** expresses that something is worth being done.

| pona tawa lukin ‚Üí pona lukin |
|:-|
| *good for the eye* ‚Üí *worth seeing*, *beautiful* |

üëç "insa (tawa)" + **tool noun** expresses that something is do-able, whereas "open (tawa)" + **tool noun** expresses that something is not obstructing to the tool implied.

| insa lukin | open lukin |
|:-|:-|
| *visible* | *transparent* |

### Simple Verbs
*(nimi suli wawa pona)*

| [‚Üë](#verbs) |
|:-|
| [Thing Verbs](#thing-verbs) |

**Simple verbs** imply "e ijo".

| alasa |
|:-|
| *to hunt* |

üëé **Simple verbs** can be used as a [modifier](#modifiers) implying **passive**.

| nimi li sona |
|:-|
| *the word is known* |

ü§î **Sonja** allows all **verbs** to imply "e ijo".

| mi open e ni lon sewi. ‚Üí mi open lon sewi. |
|:-|
| *I begin with the divine.* |

ü§î ‚Ä¶

| kama e ‚Üí pali e | open e ‚Üí pali e |
|:-|:-|
| *to build*, *make* | *to prepare* |

#### Thing Verbs
*(nimi suli wawa ijo)*

| [‚Üë](#simple-verbs) |
|:-|

**Thing verbs** are **simple verbs**. As a [noun](#nouns), they mean the verb's object.

| sitelen |
|:-|
| *to draw* ‚Üí *picture* |

### Pre-Verbs
*(nimi suli wawa poka)*

| [‚Üë](#verbs) |
|:-|

| awen |
|:-|
| *enduring* ‚Üí *to keep -ing* |

After the **pre-verbs** "awen" and "kama", "lon" can disappear.

| awen lon ‚Üí awen |
|:-|
| *to stay* |

If a **verb** is an **pre-verb**, it implies a **transitive verb** before a **subordinate clause**, which receives the main **verb** from the main [clause](#mute-nimi). In both [clauses](#mute-nimi), the subject (i.e. the words before the [particle "li"](#subject-and-predicate)) is the same.

| ona li wile e ni: ona li lon tomo. ‚Üí ona li wile lon tomo. |
|:-|
| *She needs, that she is at home.* ‚Üí *She has to be at home.* |

üëé The **pre-verb** "kama" and the [content word](#content-words) "pini" can disappear.

| ona li kama lape lon pini pi tenpo lili. ‚Üí ona li lape lon tenpo lili. |
|:-|
| *She will sleep after a short period of time. She will sleep soon. She falls asleep quickly.* |

üëé The [prepositions](#prepositions) "lon", "tan", "tawa" can become **pre-verbs**.

| mi lon moku e kili. | sina tan moku e kili. | ona li tawa moku e kili. |
|:-|:-|:-|
| *I am eating an apple.* | *You have been eating an apple.* | *She is going to eat an apple.* |

### Simple Modifiers
*(nimi suli poka pona)*

| [‚Üë](#modifiers) |
|:-|

**Simple modifiers** don't receive an additional meaning when they change their part-of-speech.

| mi wawa. | telo li wawa e mi. | wawa li lon insa. |
|:-|:-|:-|
| *I am strong.* | *Water strengthens me.* | *Strength lies within.* |

### Numbers
*(nimi suli poka nanpa)*

| [‚Üë](#modifiers) |
|:-|

üëç The [conjunction](#conjunctions) "en" can be with two **numbers**.

| sike esun tu | tenpo suno luka tu en tu tu |
|:-|:-|
| *two weeks* | *fourteen days* |

üëç By means of a **number** of a small quantity and a **number** of a big quantity one can express a quantity between zero and one.

| mi pana e wan wan pi tu tu tawa sina. | mi pana e wan mute pi tu tu tawa sina. |
|:-|:-|
| *I give you one quarter.* | *I give you three quarters.* |

üëç In a row, the one whose turn it's going to be is "nanpa kama", the one whose turn it is is "nanpa awen" and the one whose turn it has been is "nanpa pini".

| sina nanpa kama. | sina nanpa awen. | sina nanpa pini. |
|:-|:-|:-|
| *It's going to be your turn.* | *It's your turn.* | *It's been your turn.* |

ü§î The meanings of the **number** "mute" and of the [simple modifier](#simple-modifiers) "mute" can be one.

| mute |
|:-|
| *three, many* ‚Üí *three (or more)* |

ü§î With two [numbers](#numbers), the **comma** can disappear.

| palisa luka, tu ‚Üí palisa luka tu |
|:-|
| *seven fingers* |

### Prepositions
*(nimi suli poka luka)*

| [‚Üë](#modifiers) |
|:-|

**Prepositional phrases** follow **particle phrases**.

| mi pana e kili tawa sina. |
|:-|
| *I'm giving you the apple.* |

When a [modifier](#modifiers) and a **preposition** are the same, the **preposition** disappears. There can be an [interjection](#interjections) between the [verb](#verbs) and the [preposition](#prepositions).

| mi tawa tawa tomo seli. ‚Üí mi tawa tomo seli. | mi tawa ala tawa tomo seli. ‚Üí mi tawa ala tomo seli. |
|:-|:-|
| *I'm going to the kitchen.* | *I'm not going to the kitchen.* |

**Sonja** uses **prepositions** only with [verbs](#verbs). In this case, one can remove the **comma** between two **prepositions**.

| mi toki tawa sina, lon tomo seli. ‚Üí mi toki tawa sina lon tomo seli. |
|:-|
| *I'm talking to you in the kitchen.* |

In other cases, **Sonja** uses **modifiers** (or the **particle** "[pi](#genitive)") instead of **prepositions**.

| jan lon poka ‚Üí jan poka | pona tawa lukin ‚Üí pona lukin |
|:-|:-|
| *neighbour* | *beautiful* |

üëç When a [verb](#verbs) and a **preposition** are the same, the **preposition** disappears and the direct object moves to the end of the sentence.

| mi tawa e ona tawa tomo seli. ‚Üí mi tawa tomo seli e ona. | mi tawa ala e ona tawa tomo seli. ‚Üí mi tawa ala tomo seli e ona. |
|:-|:-|
| *I'm moving it to the kitchen.* | *I'm not moving it to the kitchen.* |

üëç "ijo" + [modifier](#modifiers) can express posession or topic.

| lipu ni li ijo mi. | lipu ni li toki e ijo mi. |
|:-|:-|
| *This book is mine.* | *This book is about me.* |

üëç "tawa poka" and "lon poka" can have the meaning of the experimental **preposition** "ante".

| jan li suli mute, mani li suli lili. ‚Üí jan li suli mute tawa/lon poka mani. ‚Üí jan li suli mute ante mani. |
|:-|
| *People are more important than money.* |

üëç "taso" as a [particle](#particles) is an abbreviation of "taso ni la".

| taso ni la mije en meli li pali li pilin pona. ‚Üí taso mije en meli li pali li pilin pona. |
|:-|
| *Independent from that, men and women work and are happy.* ‚Üí *But men and women work and are happy.* |

üëé In a contextual phrase (i.e. before the particle "[la](#contextual-fragment)"), **prepositions** can disappear.

| tawa mi la sina jan pona. ‚Üí mi la sina jan pona. |
|:-|
| *From my point of view, you are a good person.* |

üëé **Pije** removes the **preposition** "tawa" before "lukin".

| sina tawa lukin sama waso. ‚Üí sina lukin sama waso. |
|:-|
| *You look like a bird.* |

üëé Before "pilin", "ike tan" can disappear.
  
| sina pilin ike tan utala. ‚Üí sina pilin utala. |
|:-|
| *You are empathizing badly because of struggle.* ‚Üí *You are angry.* |

üëé "lon" + [spatial noun](#spatial-nouns) can become a **preposition**.

| mi tawa lon poka sina. ‚Üí mi tawa poka sina. |
|:-|
| *I'm going with you.* |

üëé "lon poka" can disappear.

| jan li suli mute lon poka mani. ‚Üí jan li suli mute mani. |
|:-|
| *People are more important than money.* |

üëé When a [preposition](#prepositions) becomes a [noun](#nouns), its meaning can become that of the word "ijo" after that **preposition**. **Pije** uses the **preposition** "tan" in this way.

| mi sona ala e ni: ni li tan seme. ‚Üí mi sona ala e tan. | ona li tawa e ona sama. ‚Üí ona li tawa e sama. |
|:-|:-|
| *I don't know the reason.* | *It moves itself.* |

ü§î ‚Ä¶

| a ‚Üí lon |
|:-|
| *true* |

### Simple Particles
*(nimi lili pona)*

| [‚Üë](#particles) |
|:-|

**Simple Particles** are tokiponized names. They always follow a [noun](#nouns). "pi nimi" can disappear before **simple particle**. In this case, the **quotation marks** disappear.

| ona li jo e nimi "Sonja". | jan pi nimi "Sonja" ‚Üí jan Sonja 
|:-|:-|
| *She has the name "Sonja".* | *the person called "Sonja"* ‚Üí *Sonja* |

**Sonja** removes the [particle "pi"](#genitive) before "nanpa" with a [number](#numbers). In this case, "nanpa" itself becomes part of a **simple particle phrase**.

| sina nanpa wan | toki pi nanpa wan ‚Üí toki nanpa wan |
|:-|:-|
| *You're number one.* | *the language with the number one* ‚Üí *the language number one* |

üëé **Pije** doesn't remove the [particle "pi"](#genitive) before **simple particle phrases** with "nanpa".

### Interjections
*(nimi lili kalama)*

| [‚Üë](#particles) |
|:-|

One can express an emotion by means of a plain **interjection** as if it were a **sentence**.

| a! | mu! |
|:-|:-|
| *Ha!, Wow!* | *Blah!, Meeow!* |

An **interjection** can become a [thing verb](#thing-verbs).

| soweli li kalama "mu". ‚Üí soweli li mu. | ona li kute e nimi "mu" wawa. ‚Üí ona li kute e mu wawa. |
|:-|:-|
| *The animal bleats.* | *She heard a loud bleat.* |

üëç An **interjection** can become a [modifier](#modifiers).

| a |
|:-|
| *indeed, yes* |

üëç One can reply by a phrase only, but it can't start with a **particle**.

| ona li mama. ‚Üí mama. | o jo e moku pona! ‚Üí moku pona! |
|:-|:-|
| *She is a parent.* ‚Üí *She is., Yes.* | *Have a good meal!* ‚Üí *Bon app√©tit!* |

üëç Two same words have the meaning of the [modifier](#modifiers) "a".

| sina suli a! ‚Üí sina suli suli! |
|:-|
| *You are really tall!* |

#### Subject and Predicate
*(nimi lili luka "li")*

| [‚Üë](#phrase-particles) |
|:-|

If the subject (i.e. the words before the **particle** "li") is just "mi" or "sina", the **particle** "li" disappears.

| mi li mije. ‚Üí mi mije. | sina li sin. ‚Üí sina sin. |
|:-|:-|
| *I am a man.* | *You are new.* |

A **sentence** can have multiple predicates (i.e. the words after the **particle** "li"). Their **commas** can disappear.

| ma mama li lili, li lete. ‚Üí ma mama li lili li lete. |
|:-|
| *The motherland is small and cold.* |

üëç After "mi" or "sina" the [particle "li"](#subject-and-predicate) doesn't disappear, if there is a second [particle "li"](#subject-and-predicate).

| mi lukin mute. mi sona lili. ‚Üí mi li lukin mute li sona lili. |
|:-|
| *I am seeing much and knowing little.* |

üëé **Pije** removes only the first [particle "li"](#subject-and-predicate) after "mi" or "sina".

| mi lukin mute. mi sona lili. ‚Üí mi lukin mute li sona lili. |
|:-|
| *I am seeing much and knowing little.* |

üëé When the subject (t.e. the words before the [particle "li"](#subject-and-predicate)) is one word alone, "li" disappears.

| ona li mama. ‚Üí ona mama. |
|:-|
| *She is a parent.* |

#### Direct Object
*(nimi lili luka "e")*

| [‚Üë](#phrase-particles) |
|:-|

A predicate (i.e. the words after the **particle** "li") can have multiple direct objects (i.e. the words after the **particle** "e"). Their **commas** can disappear.

| ona li seli e soweli, e pan. ‚Üí ona li seli e soweli e pan. |
|:-|
| *She cooks the meat and the rice.* |

One can express a desire by just a direct object (i.e. the words after the **particle** "e").

| tawa pona sina la mi wile e moku pona. ‚Üí moku pona! |
|:-|
| *I wish you a good meal.* ‚Üí *Bon app√©tit!* |

üëé When the [preposition](#prepositions) "kepeken" becomes a [verb](#verbs), **Pije** uses it with the **particle "e"**.

| mi kepeken ilo. ‚Üí mi kepeken e ilo. |
|:-|
| *I use the instrument.* |

ü§î The direct object of "pana" can become a [verb](#verbs).

| ona li pana e telo tawa kasi. ‚Üí ona li telo e kasi. |
|:-|
| *She gives water to the plant.* ‚Üí *She waters the plant.* |

ü§î With "utala", the [preposition](#prepositions) "tawa" can become the [particle "e"](#direct-object).

| ona li utala tawa jan ike. ‚Üí ona li utala e jan ike. |
|:-|
| *She fights the enemy.* |

#### Volative, Vocative and Imperative
*(nimi lili luka "o")*

| [‚Üë](#phrase-particles) |
|:-|

"sina" can disappear before the **particle** "o".

| sina o lukin. ‚Üí o lukin! | jan Ali o wawa. ‚Üí jan Ali o wawa! |
|:-|:-|
| *You should look.* ‚Üí *Look!* | *Ali should be strong.* ‚Üí *Ali, be strong!* |

"kute" can disappear after the **particle** "o". **Sonja** doesn't combine two "o"-**particles**.

| o kute! ‚Üí o! | jan Ali o, o wawa! ‚Üí jan Ali o wawa! |
|:-|:-|
| *Listen!* ‚Üí *Hey!* | *Hey Ali, be strong!* |

üëé A **volative** is expressed by starting the sentence with an "o".

| ni o kama: ona li lape pona. ‚Üí o ona li lape pona. |
|:-|
| *May she sleep well.* |

#### Genitive
*(nimi lili luka "pi")*

| [‚Üë](#phrase-particles) |
|:-|

If a genitive phrase (i.e. the words after the **particle** "pi") consists of only one [noun](#nouns), the **particle** "pi" disappears. So, that [noun](#nouns) becomes a [modifier](#modifiers).

| toki pi pona ‚Üí toki pona |
|:-|
| *a language of good* ‚Üí *a good language* |

As a rule of thumb, if the **particle** "pi" doesn't add useful information, it shouldn't be used.

| jan pi ijo ni ‚Üí jan ni |
|:-|
| *the person over there* ‚Üí *that person* |

üëç A genitive phrase (i.e. words after a **particle** "pi") can have another genitive phrase.

| tomo pi telo nasa pi ma ante |
|:-|
| *house of foreign beer* |

üëé **Pije** turns the [particle "pi"](#genitive) with a [pronoun](#pronouns) into a [verb](#verbs).

| ni li ijo mi. ‚Üí ni li pi mi. |
|:-|
| *This belongs to me.* ‚Üí *This is mine.* |

üëé **Pije** turns the [particle "e"](#direct-object) into the [particle "pi"](#genitive) before a noun, which means a direction.

| sina lukin e sewi. ‚Üí sina lukin sewi. |
|:-|
| *You look up.* |

üëé The [particle "e"](#direct-object) can become the [particle "pi"](#genitive).

| mi ante e toki pi sitelen tawa. ‚Üí mi ante toki e sitelen tawa. |
|:-|
| *I translate the movie.* |

üëé The [particle "pi"](#genitive) can disappear.

| tomo pi telo nasa ‚Üí tomo telo nasa |
|:-|
| *bar* |

### Conjunctions
*(nimi lili luka "en", nimi lili luka "anu")*

| [‚Üë](#transitive-particles) |
|:-|

By means of "anu seme" one can express a yes-no-question. In this case "seme" is a [verb](#verbs).

| ona li mama anu seme? |
|:-|
| *Is she a parent?* |

üëé A **comma** can become a strange **conjunction** "en".

| tomo pi telo nasa, pi ma ante ‚Üí tomo pi telo nasa en pi ma ante |
|:-|
| *foreign bar* |

üëé Every subject (i.e. the words before the [particle "li"](#subject-and-predicate) can have a strange **particle** "en".

| en sina en mi li jan lili. | en ona li jan lili. |
|:-|:-|
| *Both you and I are children.* | *She is a child.* |

üëé According to **Pije**, sentences with "anu seme" have the rude conotation of "or what".

| ona li mama anu seme? |
|:-|
| *Is she a parent or what?* |

ü§î **Sonja** uses the **particle** "en" only in order to combine multiple subjects (i.e. the words before the **particle** "li").

| toki sina en pali sina li sama. |
|:-|
| *What you say and what you do are the same.* |

ü§î In front of words and after the same words negated, the **particle** "anu" disappears.

| ona li mama ala anu mama? ‚Üí ona li mama ala mama? |
|:-|
| *Is she a parent?* |

### Contextual Fragment
*(nimi lili luka "la")*

| [‚Üë](#transitive-particles) |
|:-|

By means of the **particle "la"**, a [preposition](#prepositions) can be at the beginning of a **sentence**.

| sina pana e nimi "pona" tawa mi tan seme? ‚Üí tan seme la sina pana e nimi "pona" tawa mi? |
|:-|
| *Why are you calling me good?* |

At the beginning of a **sentence**, the [preposition](#prepositions) "lon" disappears.

| ona li kama lon tenpo pimeja ni. ‚Üí tenpo pimeja ni la ona li kama. |
|:-|
| *She is coming tonight.* ‚Üí *Tonight, she is coming.* |

If "ni" at the beginning of a **sentence** accompanies a **subordinate clause**, "ni" disappears.

| jan li pona tawa mi lon ni: mi pona tawa jan. ‚Üí mi pona tawa jan, la jan li pona tawa mi. |
|:-|
| *People are good to me, if I'm good to people.* ‚Üí *If I'm good to people, people are good to me.* |

üëé **Pije** doesn't use [prepositions](#prepositions) at the beginning of a **sentence**. But he uses strange [prepositions](#prepositions) instead.

| sina pu tan jan seme? ‚Üí tan pi jan seme la sina pu? |
|:-|
| *Because of whom do you interact with the Toki Pona book?* |
